Humanity hides underground. You found an Airlock.

What is AIRLOCK?

A post-apocalyptic survival base-builder built in Unreal Engine 5. Plan and build your underground city in a side-view ant farm, a dollhouse cutaway of every room, corridor, and district. Then drop into it in full third-person and walk the corridors you designed.

Watch your Settlers eat, sleep, work, and form relationships. Manage power, water, and ventilation. Keep people alive long enough to matter.

Above ground, the ruined city of Ashgate waits, full of loot, danger, and answers to questions you haven't thought to ask yet. Below, a community of people who depend entirely on the decisions you make.

Every module you place. Every Settler you rescue. Every surface run you take. All of it has consequences.

Why would you play it?

If you ever built a Fallout Shelter and wished you could walk inside it, this is that game. If The Last of Us made you feel something, if Subnautica made you feel small in the best possible way, if you've ever lost a character in XCOM and genuinely felt it, AIRLOCK is built for you.

You play because the base-building actually means something. The Settlers inside your bunker are not resources. They have names, classes, skills, routines, and relationships. Lose the wrong one and an entire wing goes dark.

You play because Ashgate is genuinely dangerous. Every supply run into the dead city is a risk-reward decision. Stay out too long and night falls. The Altered don't stop.

And you play because there is a story here worth finishing. One that earns its ending.

Core System

BUILD

Plan your base in the side-view ant farm, a dollhouse cutaway of your whole underground city. Then step inside and live in it. Watch your Cook prepare meals, your Medic treat the wounded, your Soldiers train. 15 districts, 150+ module types, every tier from Cave to Metal Sci-Fi.

Many modules require specific Settler classes to unlock. No Engineer, no power plant. No Medic, no infirmary. Every rescue matters. Every death costs you something.

Core System

UPGRADE

Dig out the rock. Raw cave space, sometimes with mineable ore inside. Then build. Then, when you have the Settlers and the resources, upgrade.

Each upgrade costs resources and consumes utilities. Better stats, better equipment, better mood. Five tiers: Cave, Structured Cave, Brick & Wood, Concrete & Tile, Metal Sci-Fi. Tier 0 gets you standing. Tier 4 makes the airlock a living city.

EXPLORE

Ashgate is hostile. The Altered own every inch of it. Scavenge the ruins for food, weapons, medicine, and pre-war blueprints. Every run is a gamble. Every door you open could be your last.

SURVIVE

Manage power, water, and ventilation underground. Three systems where one failure cascades into disaster. Keep your Settlers fed, rested, and sane. The base is only as strong as the people inside it.

PROTECT

Rescue Survivors from Ashgate and give them a home. They become Settlers. Manage food, water, morale, and grief. Every person has a name, a class, and skills that matter. Lose them and they're gone forever.

DISCOVER

Uncover a three-layer mystery about who you are, what happened to the world, and why the enemies hesitate around you. Memory fragments triggered by exploration change everything.

The Story

You wake up in a concrete room. Your head rings. You don't know who you are, or how you got here. On the wall in front of you, one word is scratched into the stone: KORE. You have no wallet, no ID, no memory. So it's the only name you have.

You stumble out into the ruined city of Ashgate, abandoned 300 years to The Altered. A woman finds you, scared and cautious. She's heard rumour of an underground safe place. An Airlock. She takes you there. This is where your life begins.

You build. You rescue Settlers. You survive. And as the weeks go by, fragments of memory surface. Instincts you can't explain. Voices you almost recognise. The Altered hesitate around you in ways they shouldn't. And the truth about who you are, who Kore really is, will change everything.

Three layers of revelation. Each one reframes everything you thought you knew.

The World

ASHGATE

The ruined city above your airlock. 300 years after the Sonic Nuclear War. Cities overgrown. Sky toxic. The Altered own every inch. Day is tense. Night is deadly. Survivors hide in broken shelters waiting to be found.

THE AIRLOCK

Your underground sanctuary. But it's not THE only airlock. Multiple pre-war bunkers exist across the map. Each unique. Connect them by excavating down to a buried metro station and restoring the UGA rail network. Build a civilisation, airlock by airlock.

THE ALTERED

Descendants of humans exposed to the Sonic Nuclear War. The sonic blast disrupted human brainwaves, unravelling minds while hardening bodies and stretching lifespans. Four classes, in order of intelligence and danger.

FERAL

The mindless tier. Pure aggression, no tactics, dangerous in swarms. Seven visual sub-types scatter the hordes: Husks, Hollows, Ragers, Shambles, Wretches, The Lost. Each one was a person once.

FAMILIARS

Smart enough to obey, too broken to think. Slaves to Legion. Without their leader nearby they're skittish and agitated but rarely attack. A leaderless Familiar means a Legion is somewhere close.

STALKERS

Smart, solo predators. They follow from distance, calculate whether to strike, wait for conditions to favour them. They emit a weak radiation signature your Geiger counter picks up before you see them.

LEGION

The apex threat. Fully intelligent, self-aware, centuries old. They remember the pre-war world. They have leadership, dialogue, and a hidden research programme. And they have a complicated relationship with you specifically.

The Ant Farm

AIRLOCK has two camera modes you swap between. The signature side-view ant farm, and full 3D third-person. There is no top-down. Ever.

SIDE-VIEW ANT FARM

Opened at the Airlock Computer, an in-world terminal. Camera pulls back to a dollhouse cutaway of your entire underground city. Three zoom levels: room, district, city. Every Settler visible at every zoom. Hundreds of tiny figures living their lives, all on screen at once. Plan in the ant farm.

3D PLAY MODE

Third-person over-the-shoulder. 95% of playtime. Walk the corridors you designed. Talk to Settlers face to face. Fight The Altered in Ashgate. Open doors. Eat at the mess hall. Live in the 3D city.

The Settlers

Every person you rescue from Ashgate becomes a Settler of your airlock. They have a class, a skill tree, a schedule, a personality. They live, work, sleep, form relationships, have children. Permadeath is permanent.

Fourteen classes. Each unlocks a signature module no other class can operate. No Engineer, no Power Plant. No Cook, no kitchen morale. No Elder, no Library. Your roster IS your capability ceiling.

⚔️ Soldier

Firearms, melee, tactics. Signature module: Armoury. The defence backbone.

🕵️ Scout

Stealth, scavenging, navigation. Signature: Lookout Post. Reveals the map and spots threats.

🏹 Hunter

Tracking, trapping, bows. Signature: Smokehouse. Brings home game, preserves the meat.

🎣 Fisher

Fishing, aquaculture. Signature: Aquaculture Tank. Protein without leaving the airlock.

🌾 Farmer

Crops, soil, husbandry, beekeeping. Signature: Greenhouse. The food backbone.

🔧 Engineer

Engineering, electrics, plumbing. Signature: Power Plant. Gates every Tier 2+ module.

🔫 Gunsmith

Gunsmithing, ammunition, silencer fabrication. Signature: Gunsmithing Bench. Stealth combat enabler.

⚒️ Smith

Metalwork, carpentry, masonry. Signature: Forge. Feeds half the airlock with metal.

🧵 Tailor

Tailoring, leatherwork, dyeing. Signature: Tailoring Workshop. Clothes, armour, dignity.

⚗️ Chemist

Chemistry, pharmaceuticals, explosives. Signature: Chemistry Lab. The invisible engine.

🍳 Cook

Cooking, baking, preservation, brewing. Signature: Kitchen. Morale backbone of the airlock.

❤️ Medic

First aid, surgery, herbalism. Signature: Infirmary. Saves lives that would otherwise be permadeath.

🗝️ Thief

Stealth, lockpicking, safecracking. Signature: Safehouse. The airlock's shadow side.

🧓 Elder

Teaching, leadership, UGA history. Signature: Library. Very rare. Accelerates the whole roster.

Build a Network

The Airlock is a place, not the place. Multiple airlocks exist across the map. Each unique. Each with its own starting features, unique upgrades, and maximum build scale.

Every airlock has a buried metro station at its deepest point. Excavate down, assign an Engineer Settler to restore power, reconnect the line. The UGA rail network, 300 years dormant, comes back online. You can fast-travel between every airlock you've claimed.

CLAIM · BUILD · MOVE ON

Max your starting airlock. Leave in search of another. Your previous airlocks don't vanish. They stay intact, populated, productive. You set up automated trade routes between them. One becomes your farm. One becomes your armoury. One becomes your research hub. Rebuild civilisation, one bunker at a time.

THE METRO

A UGA-era project that collapsed before most of it went active. The tunnels are intact. The trains are not. You bring it back online, station by station, airlock by airlock. A literal thread stitching the dead map back together.

Base Building

Build in the side-view ant farm, then explore your creation in full 3D. 15 districts, 150+ module types, five tiers from raw Cave to Metal Sci-Fi.

Companions

Infrastructure

The Altered

300 years after the Sonic Nuclear War, they own the surface. Fast, relentless, and terrifyingly numerous. They hunt by sound and movement. Day gives you a window. Night belongs to them.

🗺

Open World

Ruined cities and countryside. Dynamic day/night cycle. The surface rewards the brave and punishes the reckless.

🚗

Vehicles

Scout bikes, salvage trucks, armoured carriers. Fuel is finite. Noise attracts enemies.

🐔

Animal Farming

Chickens, cattle, sheep, pigs, bees, and bioluminescent cave creatures underground.

💭

Memory System

Fragments of your past triggered by places, objects, and people. Early ones confuse. Late ones devastate.

Generational

Settlers form relationships. Have children. A generation born underground who have never seen the sky.

Specialists

Your base is only as capable as the Settlers inside it. Certain modules can only be unlocked and operated by the right class. No Engineer, no power plant. No Medic, no infirmary. Who you rescue decides what you can build.

Skill System

Settlers aren't static. They learn, improve, and specialise across 17 skill parents and 130+ sub-skills. Eight tiers from Beginner to Master, 80 levels per skill. Skills grow through use, books, teachers, and research. A rookie Scout becomes a seasoned expedition leader.

Farming

Grow crops in underground greenhouses and tend animals above and below ground. Food variety affects morale. A bad harvest hits harder than a horde. Keep your people fed or watch everything else unravel.

Combat

The Altered are fast, unpredictable, and they own the night. Fight when you have to, run when you don't. Equip your Settlers with scavenged weapons, assign roles, and know when a mission is worth the risk.

Research

Blueprints found in Ashgate unlock new modules, upgrades, and equipment back at base. Assign Settlers to research benches to accelerate progress. Some of the most powerful discoveries are hidden in the most dangerous places.

Morale and Needs

Settlers have needs beyond food and water. Grief, fear, boredom, and isolation are real threats. Build recreation rooms, libraries, music halls, memorial walls. Assign counsellors. Let people rest. A collapsing morale can end a run faster than any horde.

Salvage and Crafting

Nothing on the surface goes to waste. Strip ruins for metal, fabric, electronics, and fuel. Break down salvage into components. Craft weapons, tools, and module parts back at base. Every run should come home with something.

Phase 1 - Blockout & Prototype
✓ Complete
This is where it all started. Built a military compound blockout in Unreal Engine 5 to prove the concept actually works. Integrated the ALS (Advanced Locomotion System) for third-person character control. Created a prototype dollhouse camera to test the core vision: design underground from a side-view ant farm cutaway, then drop into your creation in full 3D and walk the corridors you built. Walk through the corridors you built. See the rooms you placed. The question was simple: does it feel right? It did. That was the moment AIRLOCK stopped being an idea and started being a game.
Phase 2 - Grid System V1
✓ Complete
The backbone of the entire game. Built the GridManager system in C++ from scratch - world-to-grid coordinate conversion, cell states (empty, occupied, reserved), multi-floor support for stacking levels underground. Modules snap to the grid with adjacency detection - walls and doors auto-generate between rooms. This is the system everything else is built on top of.
Phase 3 - Character V1
✓ Complete
Took the ALS system apart and rebuilt it for AIRLOCK. Customised movement feel, camera behaviour, and transitions between underground and surface modes. The character controller handles tight corridors and wide-open surface exploration seamlessly - two very different feels, one character.
Phase 4 - UI V1
✓ Complete
Built the full main menu system from scratch - epilepsy warning, brightness calibration, press-any-key splash, main menu with all sub-screens and settings. Custom-designed with a worn military aesthetic that feels like it belongs in a bunker.
Phase 5 - HUD V1
✓ Complete
In-game HUD built: health, stamina, compass, status effects, and notifications. Full inventory system - equip weapons, fire, swap, drop, pick up. Items degrade over time. Nothing lasts forever in AIRLOCK.
Phase 6 - Combat V1
✓ Complete
Weapons you can equip, fire, and feel. Ranged and melee integrated with the inventory system - every weapon degrades with use and can break. Ammunition is scarce. Combat is designed to feel desperate, not heroic. You're not a soldier - you're a survivor.
Phase 7 - Modules V1
✓ Complete
The module system fully integrated into the HUD - browse 14+ categories, see every detail before you place (tier, size, morale bonus, build time, power draw, water usage). Place, move, remove. Watch your Settlers use what you build. It's not just building, it's bringing your base to life.
Phase 8 - Hordes V1
✓ Complete
An optimised horde system - large numbers of The Altered on screen without tanking performance. AI states: idle wanderers, alerted searchers, full-speed chasers, swarming attackers. They hunt. They swarm. They overwhelm. Daytime is tense. Night is deadly.
Phase 9 - Loot V1
✓ Complete
Lootable items scattered throughout ruined buildings, abandoned vehicles, and the remains of those who didn't make it. Food, weapons, medicine, blueprints, raw materials, rare components. Every surface trip is a risk-reward calculation - push deeper for better loot, or play it safe near the airlock?
Phase 10 - Character V2 (MetaHuman)
✓ Complete
Out with the placeholder, in with the real thing. The ALS mannequin replaced with a fully assembled MetaHuman character - realistic face, hair, skin, expressions. The foundation for cutscenes, emotional storytelling, and characters you actually care about.
Phase 11 - Companion V1
✓ Complete
You shouldn't face the surface alone. Your companion follows you - engaging enemies, sniffing out hidden loot, reacting to danger before you see it. They have personality. They have moods. They remember. Meet Marv.
Phase 12 - Tier System V1
⧗ In Progress
Every module in the game can be upgraded through four visual and functional tiers: Cave, Brick, Concrete, and Sci-fi. Each upgrade costs resources, consumes utilities, and delivers real improvements - better stats, better equipment access, better mood for the people living inside. Building the tier progression system and the upgrade flow that makes every improvement feel earned.
Phase 13 - Settlers V1
→ Planned
Settlers aren't furniture. Each one has a name, class, backstory, skill tree, and daily routine. 14 classes (Soldier, Scout, Hunter, Fisher, Farmer, Engineer, Gunsmith, Smith, Tailor, Chemist, Cook, Medic, Thief, Elder). They eat in the canteen, sleep in their assigned room, work their assigned module, socialise in the common area. They form relationships, develop opinions, have children, react to loss. The base only comes alive when the people inside it do.
Phase 14 - Infrastructure V1
→ Planned
Power, Water, and Ventilation - three interconnected systems that your entire base depends on. A generator failure cuts lights and shuts down modules. A burst pipe floods a corridor. A ventilation failure poisons the air. These aren't background mechanics - they're the heartbeat of your base. Building, placing, and managing them is the difference between a thriving colony and a slow collapse.
Phase 15 - City & Story V1
→ Planned
Turn the sandbox into a world with purpose. A fully explorable ruined city - streets, overgrown buildings, collapsed infrastructure. Key points of interest drive the story forward. The narrative framework: three-layer reveal, memory triggers, environmental storytelling, the opening sequence. The city is where the lore lives - in graffiti on walls, in notes left behind, in the bones of a civilisation that didn't survive.
Phase 16 - Playable Demo
→ Target 2026
A 60+ minute guided experience. Wake up alone in a concrete room. A word scratched on the wall: KORE. Escape into Ashgate. Discover an airlock. Build your first rooms. Rescue your first Survivors. Watch them become Settlers. Venture back for supplies. Survive the night. The demo ends with a hook that makes you need to know what happens next. This goes straight onto Steam for the world to play.

Phase 15 - Expanded Customisation
→ More Everything
More modules. More base areas. More ways to personalise your underground world. Higher-tier kitchens, advanced farms, specialised workshops, recreation rooms, schools for children born underground. When your base maxes out, it's not game over - find a new location, start again bigger and better. Your first base becomes a node in something larger.
Phase 16 - Trading & Economy
→ A Living Economy
Set up automated trade routes between your airlocks. Previous airlocks you've moved on from don't sit empty, they become trading partners. Barter, buy, sell with NPC vendors. Supply and demand shifts based on scarcity. Surplus food but need medicine? Another airlock has the opposite problem. Trade routes emerge. Phase 17 - Multi-Airlock System
→ Expand Your Network
One airlock isn't enough. Discover new underground locations: pre-war military bunkers, research annexes, mine shafts, civic shelters. Each has unique starting features and unique upgrades. Build, max out, leave, find another. Your previous airlocks stay intact and active. Specialise them: your farm, your armoury, your research hub. Rebuild civilisation, one airlock at a time.
Phase 18 - Underground Metro
→ Connect Everything
Every airlock has a buried metro station at its deepest point. Excavate down through the grid. Clear the rubble. Assign an Engineer Settler to restore power. Reconnect the line to the wider UGA rail network. Once active, fast-travel between your airlocks without touching Ashgate. Supplies move. Settlers transfer where needed. You ride the rails through dark tunnels beneath a dead world. It's infrastructure. It's logistics. It's the spine of your underground civilisation.

Dev Diary

Follow the development of AIRLOCK from the very beginning. Every win, every setback, every breakthrough - documented honestly.

17 April 2026
The biggest canon lockdown day in the project's history. Locked the full Altered class breakdown (Feral with 7 visual sub-types, Familiars, Stalkers, Legion with Geiger-counter mechanic). Locked the 14 Settler classes and their signature modules. Locked the skill system at 8 tiers × 10 sub-levels (80 total levels per skill), 17 parent categories, 130+ sub-skills, with full XP propagation rules (100% sub + 10% parent trickle + 10% cross-skill bleed). Locked the multi-airlock + metro network as core design, not late-game. Locked the ant farm + dual-view camera system. Wrote USkillComponent and SkillTypes in C++. Wrote the AirlockSkillXPWidget popup. Updated performance config for Lumen software-RT. Steamworks account registered (phase 1 done). Updated every section of this website. Rest well. Tomorrow, we ship.
14 April 2026
Website visual overhaul complete. The site has been fully redesigned to match AIRLOCK's in-game UI aesthetic: dark warm palette, cream and bone-white text, grey-forward accents, torn/damaged edges on panels. All blues are gone. Buttons now look like aged parchment, matching the in-game module UI exactly. All 8 dev diary videos embedded. The website finally feels like the game.
13 April 2026
Major codebase refactor complete: DT_Modules is now the single source of truth for all module data. Previously, every Blueprint had its own duplicate display name, description, tier stats, power draw, morale bonus and build time - now all of that lives in the Data Table. The UI panels (Selected, Upgrade, Compare) now pull directly from DT_Modules first, falling back to Blueprint properties only if no DT entry exists. Cleaner, faster, and far easier to balance.
13 April 2026
Save/load tier persistence bug fixed. Modules were always spawning at tier 0 on load, regardless of what tier they were at when saved. Root cause: relying on UE's SaveGame property specifier, which has no effect in AIRLOCK's manual struct-based save system. Fixed by adding CurrentTier to the FPlacedModuleSaveData struct, capturing it from the live actor at save time, and restoring it via SetTier() on load. Tiers now survive a full save/load cycle correctly.
13 April 2026
Diversion VCS crisis averted. Accidentally submitted changes to a review draft instead of committing directly - the workspace reverted to commit 10 and all recent changes appeared lost. Tracked down the draft review on app.diversion.dev, found it had already auto-merged as dv.commit.12, created a LatestFixes branch from that commit, then merged it back into main (now at dv.commit.13). All 329 changed files restored. Nothing lost.
9 April 2026
Website goes live at theairlock.co.uk! Built from scratch - single-page HTML with particle effects, interactive 27-phase roadmap, dev diary section, and embedded music player. Hosted on Cloudflare Pages via GitHub. Domain connected. The game finally has a home on the internet.
9 April 2026
MetaHuman character (MH_Adrien) successfully assembled in UE5 after fixing a Derived Data Cache issue that was eating 256GB of disk space. Redirected DDC to D: drive. Next step: retarget ALS animations onto the MetaHuman skeleton and swap out the placeholder character.
9 April 2026
Full development roadmap finalised - 27 phases from prototype to multiplayer. Four stages: Building the Demo (12 phases, 7 complete), Funding & Launch (Kickstarter → Early Access → Narrative), Expanding the World (Customisation → Trading → Multi-Base → Metro), and Global Maps (UK, Japan, USA, Germany, Russia, Australia, Brazil → Full Release).
4 April 2026
UE5 project settings locked. Async level streaming working. Demo roadmap created - 56 tasks across 8 sprints.
3 April 2026
Surface world pipeline working end-to-end - massive ruined city environment now loading as a playable level. Module system expanded to 14 categories. Full 60-minute demo gameplay loop documented.

Join My Journey

I'm Henry. I've spent over a decade as a professional Environment Artist and Art Lead in the games industry, working at studios including Rebellion, nDreams, Offworld Industries, and Ubisoft. I've helped ship AAA and indie titles. I know exactly what it takes to bring a game from concept to completion, because I've been doing it my entire career.

But throughout all of it, I've carried one ambition: to build something of my own. Not someone else's vision. Mine. A game that pulls together everything I love about the medium: deep base-building, survival tension, real characters, and a story with genuine emotional weight. AIRLOCK has lived in my head for years. Every studio, every shipped title, every hard lesson has shaped what it's becoming.

I won't pretend this is my first attempt at crowdfunding. I've tried Kickstarter before, and those projects failed. Looking back, they deserved to. The ideas weren't developed enough, the games weren't far enough along, and I was asking people to believe in something that wasn't yet real. I wasn't ready.

This time is different. AIRLOCK already has a working prototype built in Unreal Engine 5: a C++ grid-based building system, functional module placement, save and load, a full UI prototype, detailed design documentation, and a registered studio under Ironbridge Games LTD. This is no longer just an idea. It's an active, playable project with real momentum behind it.

I have a wife and six kids. I have a day job. Every hour spent on AIRLOCK comes from late nights, early mornings, and sacrificed weekends. I want to dedicate myself to this full-time, not because it's a fantasy, but because I genuinely believe I have the experience, the skillset, and the vision to make it happen.

The goal is straightforward: Kickstarter funds the development. Once the demo is ready, it goes onto Steam Early Access so you can play it, break it, give feedback, and help shape what gets built next. This isn't a closed-door project. I want AIRLOCK developed alongside the people who believe in it, through dev diaries, Discord, and honest progress every step of the way.

Every backer, every follower, every person who joins and says "I want to see this happen" pushes it one step closer to reality. That's what keeps me going at 2am after the kids are asleep.
