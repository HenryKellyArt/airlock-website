Humanity hides underground. You found an Airlock.

What is AIRLOCK?

A post-apocalyptic survival base-builder built in Unreal Engine 5. Plan and construct your underground bunker from above in top-down mode, then drop into it in full third-person and walk the corridors you built.

Watch your survivors eat, sleep, work, and form relationships. Manage power, water, and ventilation. Keep people alive long enough to matter.

Above ground, a ruined open world waits, full of loot, danger, and answers to questions you haven't thought to ask yet. Below, a colony of people who depend entirely on the decisions you make.

Every module you place. Every survivor you recruit. Every surface run you take. All of it has consequences.

Why would you play it?

If you ever built a Fallout Shelter and wished you could walk inside it, this is that game. If The Last of Us made you feel something, if Subnautica made you feel small in the best possible way, if you've ever lost a character in XCOM and genuinely felt it, AIRLOCK is built for you.

You play because the base-building actually means something. The people inside your bunker are not resources. They have names, skills, routines, and relationships. Lose the wrong one and an entire wing goes dark.

You play because the surface is genuinely dangerous. Every supply run is a risk-reward decision. Stay out too long and night falls. The Altered don't stop.

And you play because there is a story here worth finishing. One that earns its ending.

Core System

BUILD

Plan your base in dollhouse view, then step inside and live in it. Watch your cook prepare meals, your medic treat the wounded, your soldiers train. 14+ module categories: Power, Water, Ventilation, Farming, Medical, Research, and Military.

Many modules require specific survivors to unlock. Lose the wrong person and an entire wing goes dark. Every recruit matters. Every death costs you something.

Core System

UPGRADE

Dig out the rock. Raw cave space, sometimes with mineable goods inside. Then build. Then, when you have the survivors and the resources, upgrade.

Each upgrade costs resources and consumes utilities. Better stats, better equipment, better mood. Four tiers: Cave, Brick, Concrete, Sci-fi. Tier 0 gets you standing. Tier 3 keeps you thriving.

EXPLORE

The surface is hostile. The Altered own every inch of it. Scavenge the ruins for food, weapons, medicine, and blueprints. Every run is a gamble. Every door you open could be your last.

SURVIVE

Manage power, water, and ventilation underground. Three systems where one failure cascades into disaster. Keep your people fed, rested, and sane. The base is only as strong as the people inside it.

PROTECT

Rescue survivors and give them a home. Manage food, water, morale, and grief. Every person has a name, a story, and skills that matter. Lose them and they're gone forever.

DISCOVER

Uncover a three-layer mystery about who you are, what happened to the world, and why the enemies hesitate around you. Memory fragments triggered by exploration change everything.

The Story

You wake up. Alarms blaring. A dim room: medical equipment, dead monitors, restraints on the bed. You don't know who you are. You stumble through corridors lined with bodies, out into a world consumed by 300 years of ruin.

A dead man in a jeep. A satnav with a destination. You drive. That destination is an Airlock, an underground shelter. This is where your life begins. But as you build, rescue, and survive... fragments of memory surface. And the truth about who you are will change everything.

Three layers of revelation. Each one reframes everything you thought you knew.

Key Features

⚙

Base Building

Build in dollhouse view, then explore your creation in full 3D. 14+ module categories. Five tiers from cave dirt to sci-fi metal.

Explore Your Base

Step inside your creation and walk every corridor. Your survivors eat, sleep, work, and grieve in real time. It's not a management screen - it's home.

Upgrade Your Base

Dig out the space, mine what's inside, then build. Upgrade through four tiers: Cave, Brick, Concrete, Sci-fi. Every upgrade costs resources and consumes utilities. Every upgrade pays off.

Companions

You don't survive alone. Companions join your journey on the surface, each with their own story, abilities, and reasons for being there. Some will surprise you.

👥

Survivor Management

Every survivor has a name, background, and skills that unlock new modules. Permadeath means every loss is permanent.

Infrastructure

The Altered

300 years after the Sonic Nuclear War, they own the surface. Fast, relentless, and terrifyingly numerous. They hunt by sound and movement. Day gives you a window. Night belongs to them.

🗺

Open World

Ruined cities and countryside. Dynamic day/night cycle. The surface rewards the brave and punishes the reckless.

🚗

Vehicles

Scout bikes, salvage trucks, armoured carriers. Fuel is finite. Noise attracts enemies.

🐔

Animal Farming

Chickens, cattle, sheep, pigs, bees, and bioluminescent cave creatures underground.

💭

Memory System

Fragments of your past triggered by places, objects, and people. Early ones confuse. Late ones devastate.

Generational

Survivors form relationships. Have children. A generation born underground who have never seen the sky.

Specialists

Your base is only as capable as the people inside it. Certain modules can only be unlocked and operated by survivors with the right skills. No engineer, no power plant. No doctor, no clinic. Who you rescue decides what you can build.

Skill System

Survivors aren't static. They learn, improve, and specialise over time. A rookie scavenger becomes a seasoned scout. A basic cook becomes a nutritionist keeping morale high. Skills grow through use and unlock new possibilities across the base and on the surface.

Farming

Grow crops in underground greenhouses and tend animals above and below ground. Food variety affects morale. A bad harvest hits harder than a horde. Keep your people fed or watch everything else unravel.

Combat

The Altered are fast, unpredictable, and they own the night. Fight when you have to, run when you don't. Equip your survivors with scavenged weapons, assign roles, and know when a mission is worth the risk.

Research

Blueprints found on the surface unlock new modules, upgrades, and equipment back at base. Assign survivors to research to accelerate progress. Some of the most powerful discoveries are hidden in the most dangerous places.

Morale and Needs

Survivors have needs beyond food and water. Grief, fear, boredom, and isolation are real threats. Build recreation rooms, assign counsellors, let people rest. A collapsing morale can end a run faster than any horde.

Salvage and Crafting

Nothing on the surface goes to waste. Strip ruins for metal, fabric, electronics, and fuel. Break down salvage into components. Craft weapons, tools, and module parts back at base. Every run should come home with something.

Phase 1 - Blockout & Prototype
✓ Complete
This is where it all started. Built a military compound blockout in Unreal Engine 5 to prove the concept actually works. Integrated the ALS (Advanced Locomotion System) for third-person character control. Created a prototype dollhouse camera to test the core vision - build underground in top-down, then drop into your creation in full 3D and explore it. Walk through the corridors you built. See the rooms you placed. The question was simple: does it feel right? It did. That was the moment AIRLOCK stopped being an idea and started being a game.
Phase 2 - Grid System V1
✓ Complete
The backbone of the entire game. Built the GridManager system in C++ from scratch - world-to-grid coordinate conversion, cell states (empty, occupied, reserved), multi-floor support for stacking levels underground. Modules snap to the grid with adjacency detection - walls and doors auto-generate between rooms. This is the system everything else is built on top of.
Phase 3 - Character V1
✓ Complete
Took the ALS system apart and rebuilt it for AIRLOCK. Customised movement feel, camera behaviour, and transitions between underground and surface modes. The character controller handles tight corridors and wide-open surface exploration seamlessly - two very different feels, one character.
Phase 4 - UI V1
✓ Complete
Built the full main menu system from scratch - epilepsy warning, brightness calibration, press-any-key splash, main menu with all sub-screens and settings. Custom-designed with a worn military aesthetic that feels like it belongs in a bunker.
Phase 5 - HUD V1
✓ Complete
In-game HUD built: health, stamina, compass, status effects, and notifications. Full inventory system - equip weapons, fire, swap, drop, pick up. Items degrade over time. Nothing lasts forever in AIRLOCK.
Phase 6 - Combat V1
✓ Complete
Weapons you can equip, fire, and feel. Ranged and melee integrated with the inventory system - every weapon degrades with use and can break. Ammunition is scarce. Combat is designed to feel desperate, not heroic. You're not a soldier - you're a survivor.
Phase 7 - Modules V1
✓ Complete
The module system fully integrated into the HUD - browse 14+ categories, see every detail before you place (tier, size, morale bonus, build time, power draw, water usage). Place, move, remove. Watch your survivors use what you build. It's not just building - it's bringing your base to life.
Phase 8 - Hordes V1
✓ Complete
An optimised horde system - large numbers of The Altered on screen without tanking performance. AI states: idle wanderers, alerted searchers, full-speed chasers, swarming attackers. They hunt. They swarm. They overwhelm. Daytime is tense. Night is deadly.
Phase 9 - Loot V1
✓ Complete
Lootable items scattered throughout ruined buildings, abandoned vehicles, and the remains of those who didn't make it. Food, weapons, medicine, blueprints, raw materials, rare components. Every surface trip is a risk-reward calculation - push deeper for better loot, or play it safe near the airlock?
Phase 10 - Character V2 (MetaHuman)
✓ Complete
Out with the placeholder, in with the real thing. The ALS mannequin replaced with a fully assembled MetaHuman character - realistic face, hair, skin, expressions. The foundation for cutscenes, emotional storytelling, and characters you actually care about.
Phase 11 - Companion V1
✓ Complete
You shouldn't face the surface alone. Your companion follows you - engaging enemies, sniffing out hidden loot, reacting to danger before you see it. They have personality. They have moods. They remember. Meet Marv.
Phase 12 - Tier System V1
⧗ In Progress
Every module in the game can be upgraded through four visual and functional tiers: Cave, Brick, Concrete, and Sci-fi. Each upgrade costs resources, consumes utilities, and delivers real improvements - better stats, better equipment access, better mood for the people living inside. Building the tier progression system and the upgrade flow that makes every improvement feel earned.
Phase 13 - NPCs V1
→ Planned
Survivors aren't furniture. Each NPC has a name, backstory, skill set, and daily routine. They eat in the canteen, sleep in their assigned room, work their assigned module, socialise in the common area. They form relationships, develop opinions, and react to loss. The base only comes alive when the people inside it do.
Phase 14 - Infrastructure V1
→ Planned
Power, Water, and Ventilation - three interconnected systems that your entire base depends on. A generator failure cuts lights and shuts down modules. A burst pipe floods a corridor. A ventilation failure poisons the air. These aren't background mechanics - they're the heartbeat of your base. Building, placing, and managing them is the difference between a thriving colony and a slow collapse.
Phase 15 - City & Story V1
→ Planned
Turn the sandbox into a world with purpose. A fully explorable ruined city - streets, overgrown buildings, collapsed infrastructure. Key points of interest drive the story forward. The narrative framework: three-layer reveal, memory triggers, environmental storytelling, the opening sequence. The city is where the lore lives - in graffiti on walls, in notes left behind, in the bones of a civilisation that didn't survive.
Phase 16 - Playable Demo
→ Target 2026
A 60+ minute guided experience. Wake up alone. Escape the surface. Discover the airlock. Build your first rooms. Rescue survivors. Watch your base come alive. Venture back for supplies. Survive the night. The demo ends with a hook that makes you need to know what happens next. This goes straight onto Steam for the world to play.

Phase 15 - Expanded Customisation
→ More Everything
More modules. More base areas. More ways to personalise your underground world. Higher-tier kitchens, advanced farms, specialised workshops, recreation rooms, schools for children born underground. When your base maxes out, it's not game over - find a new location, start again bigger and better. Your first base becomes a node in something larger.
Phase 16 - Trading & Economy
→ A Living Economy
You're not alone underground. Other airlocks exist - NPC settlements with their own survivors, needs, and resources. A full trading system: barter, buy, sell with vendors. Visit other people's airlocks and see how they built their communities. A banking system tracks your wealth. Supply and demand shifts based on scarcity. Surplus food but need medicine? Phase 17 - Multi-Base System

One base isn't enough. Discover new underground locations - abandoned bunkers, collapsed metro stations, forgotten military installations. Each becomes a new base. Build it up. Staff it with survivors. Specialise it - your farm, your armoury, your trading hub. Fast travel between bases as your network grows. You're not just surviving anymore. You're rebuilding civilisation, one airlock at a time.
Phase 18 - Underground Metro
→ Connect Everything
Build a metro connecting your bases underground. Clear collapsed stations. Lay tracks. Restore power. Survivors travel between airlocks without touching the surface. Supplies move, survivors transfer where needed, and you ride the rails through dark tunnels beneath a dead world. It's infrastructure. It's logistics. It's the spine of your underground civilisation.

Dev Diary

Follow the development of AIRLOCK from the very beginning. Every win, every setback, every breakthrough - documented honestly.

14 April 2026
Website visual overhaul complete. The site has been fully redesigned to match AIRLOCK's in-game UI aesthetic - dark warm palette, cream and bone-white text, amber accents, torn/damaged edges on panels. All blues are gone. Buttons now look like aged parchment, matching the in-game module UI exactly. All 8 dev diary videos embedded. The website finally feels like the game.
13 April 2026
Major codebase refactor complete: DT_Modules is now the single source of truth for all module data. Previously, every Blueprint had its own duplicate display name, description, tier stats, power draw, morale bonus and build time - now all of that lives in the Data Table. The UI panels (Selected, Upgrade, Compare) now pull directly from DT_Modules first, falling back to Blueprint properties only if no DT entry exists. Cleaner, faster, and far easier to balance.
13 April 2026
Save/load tier persistence bug fixed. Modules were always spawning at tier 0 on load, regardless of what tier they were at when saved. Root cause: relying on UE's SaveGame property specifier, which has no effect in AIRLOCK's manual struct-based save system. Fixed by adding CurrentTier to the FPlacedModuleSaveData struct, capturing it from the live actor at save time, and restoring it via SetTier() on load. Tiers now survive a full save/load cycle correctly.
13 April 2026
Diversion VCS crisis averted. Accidentally submitted changes to a review draft instead of committing directly - the workspace reverted to commit 10 and all recent changes appeared lost. Tracked down the draft review on app.diversion.dev, found it had already auto-merged as dv.commit.12, created a LatestFixes branch from that commit, then merged it back into main (now at dv.commit.13). All 329 changed files restored. Nothing lost.
9 April 2026
Website goes live at theairlock.co.uk! Built from scratch - single-page HTML with particle effects, interactive 27-phase roadmap, dev diary section, and embedded music player. Hosted on Cloudflare Pages via GitHub. Domain connected. The game finally has a home on the internet.
9 April 2026
MetaHuman character (MH_Adrien) successfully assembled in UE5 after fixing a Derived Data Cache issue that was eating 256GB of disk space. Redirected DDC to D: drive. Next step: retarget ALS animations onto the MetaHuman skeleton and swap out the placeholder character.
9 April 2026
Full development roadmap finalised - 27 phases from prototype to multiplayer. Four stages: Building the Demo (12 phases, 7 complete), Funding & Launch (Kickstarter → Early Access → Narrative), Expanding the World (Customisation → Trading → Multi-Base → Metro), and Global Maps (UK, Japan, USA, Germany, Russia, Australia, Brazil → Full Release).
4 April 2026
UE5 project settings locked. Async level streaming working. Demo roadmap created - 56 tasks across 8 sprints.
3 April 2026
Surface world pipeline working end-to-end - massive ruined city environment now loading as a playable level. Module system expanded to 14 categories. Full 60-minute demo gameplay loop documented.

Join My Journey

I'm Henry. I've spent over a decade as a professional Environment Artist and Art Lead in the games industry, working at studios including Rebellion, nDreams, Offworld Industries, and Ubisoft. I've helped ship AAA and indie titles. I know exactly what it takes to bring a game from concept to completion, because I've been doing it my entire career.

But throughout all of it, I've carried one ambition: to build something of my own. Not someone else's vision. Mine. A game that pulls together everything I love about the medium: deep base-building, survival tension, real characters, and a story with genuine emotional weight. AIRLOCK has lived in my head for years. Every studio, every shipped title, every hard lesson has shaped what it's becoming.

I won't pretend this is my first attempt at crowdfunding. I've tried Kickstarter before, and those projects failed. Looking back, they deserved to. The ideas weren't developed enough, the games weren't far enough along, and I was asking people to believe in something that wasn't yet real. I wasn't ready.

This time is different. AIRLOCK already has a working prototype built in Unreal Engine 5: a C++ grid-based building system, functional module placement, save and load, a full UI prototype, detailed design documentation, and a registered studio under Ironbridge Games LTD. This is no longer just an idea. It's an active, playable project with real momentum behind it.

I have a wife and six kids. I have a day job. Every hour spent on AIRLOCK comes from late nights, early mornings, and sacrificed weekends. I want to dedicate myself to this full-time, not because it's a fantasy, but because I genuinely believe I have the experience, the skillset, and the vision to make it happen.

The goal is straightforward: Kickstarter funds the development. Once the demo is ready, it goes onto Steam Early Access so you can play it, break it, give feedback, and help shape what gets built next. This isn't a closed-door project. I want AIRLOCK developed alongside the people who believe in it, through dev diaries, Discord, and honest progress every step of the way.

Every backer, every follower, every person who joins and says "I want to see this happen" pushes it one step closer to reality. That's what keeps me going at 2am after the kids are asleep.
